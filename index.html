Commentary and Insights

What the data reveals about where technology and startups are heading

🤖

The Age of AI Agents

AI agent companies grew from 13% of YC batches in W2023 to over 25% by W2026. Agents for sales, finance ops, legal review, HR, customer support, and coding have all exploded — YC is betting AI will replace entire job functions.

⚔️

Defense and National Security Surge

Defense AI went from 0.3% to 1.6% of all YC companies, a 5x relative increase. Physical-world AI with strategic importance is now a priority driven by geopolitical tensions and defense modernization.

🏭

The Physical World Turn

Manufacturing AI and industrial robotics nearly doubled as a share of YC investments. The atoms-not-bits movement is real — AI for factories, warehouses, and physical operations represents the next major wave.

💊

Bio is Back

Drug discovery AI, clinical workflow automation, and healthcare diagnostics have all grown consistently. Foundation models plus biology created a new generation of biotech startups different from the CRISPR era.

🔧

Dev Tools Maturing

AI coding tools declined slightly as the market matures. The coding assistant space is commoditizing. YC is now funding second-order plays: AI for QA, security testing, code review, and DevOps.

🌍

Global Fintech Proliferating

Fintech remains 8-12% per batch with a new character: stablecoins, crypto infra, and cross-border payment rails making a comeback. The theme is programmable money for the developing world.

🚀

The Vertical AI Playbook

The dominant startup archetype of 2024-2026: take a specific industry and apply an AI agent layer automating the most expensive human workflows. This playbook is being applied at scale.

🔮

What Comes Next

Signals in the most recent batches: AI-native hardware products, scientific discovery AI for physics and materials, multi-agent orchestration infrastructure, and AI companies in overlooked geographies.
